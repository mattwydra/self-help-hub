Finding Your Direction: Goal Setting Guide

Goal setting is a skill that takes practice. If you're not sure where to start, this guide can help you discover meaningful goals aligned with your purpose.

1. Discovering Your Natural Talents

According to Robert Greene in "Mastery," we all have natural inclinations that point toward our life's work.

Reflect on these questions:

  • What activities did you enjoy as a child, before external pressures influenced you?
  • What tasks or subjects do you find yourself naturally drawn to?
  • When do you experience "flow" - where time seems to disappear?
  • What work would you do even if you weren't paid for it?

2. Identifying Your Values

Goals aligned with your core values are more likely to motivate you.

Ask yourself:

  • What principles are non-negotiable in your life?
  • When have you felt most proud of yourself?
  • What would you want people to say about you at your 80th birthday celebration?
  • What issues or causes move you emotionally?

3. Finding Your Ideal Future

Visualization helps uncover meaningful goals.

Imagine:

  • If you had unlimited resources and couldn't fail, what would you do?
  • What does your ideal day look like 3 years from now?
  • What achievements would make you feel fulfilled?
  • What skills or knowledge would you love to master?

4. The "Apprenticeship Path" (from Mastery)

Robert Greene suggests that mastery comes from a deep learning phase followed by creative innovation.

Consider:

  • What field or domain feels like it could be your "life's task"?
  • Who could be your mentors in this area?
  • What skills would you need to develop over the next 3 years?
  • How could you add your unique perspective to this field?

Start with one area that resonates most strongly with you. It's better to deeply pursue one meaningful goal than to spread yourself thin across many.
